Reflection blog on Unit 3



In this unit, I learned what failure actually is and how the great man deals with it. Before this, I saw failure as something else, like shame, to avoid, hide, or feel embarrassed about. I believed that failure meant not being good enough. However, after reading this chapter, my perspective on failure has completely changed.

One concept that stuck in my mind was "intelligent failure". This means failing to clearly define purpose, proper research, and a meaningful goal. It is not about making careless mistakes, but about taking thought risks. When we fail in this way, we get only valuable insights that help us improve.

There is one beautiful quote by Thomas Edison

that is, "I have not failed. I have just found 10000 ways that won't work. This article inspires me to keep working on my goal even in times of failure because it is the first step towards success.
