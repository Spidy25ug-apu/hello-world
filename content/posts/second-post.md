Reflection on Unit 2 

In Unit 2, I studied the BRIS, where I explored the wide range of topics that helped me to understand how mathematics and computers are connected. At the beginning, it seemed technical, but gradually they became meaningful as I practiced on them. I have learned about the number systems, that is, binary, decimal, and hexadecimal conversions. Initially, it was difficult and confusing, but with practice, it was easy. I got to know how computers actually perform calculations at a fundamental level. Another topic that grabbed my attention was fun with images. I know what an image is, but not in detail, not more. learning that images are made up of pixels, each pixel holds a value, was fascinating. It changed my mind about how I looked at digital images. I learned how image quality depends on image resolution. It was challenging for me to understand how flipping or resizing happens based on logical operations. Moving ahead, I knew that optimization and formal analysis helped me think logically, instead of solving problems directly. These topics I found difficult and confusing. The Bridges of Konigsberg problem was good but not simple. It showed how real-world puzzles led to development of graph theory. Finally, learning about abstraction and modeling taught me how to simplify complex problems. Overall, I say that this kind of learning improved my problem-solving skills at some point.
