The formula for the data collection is new to me, and I haven’t used it in an Excel sheet. Sometimes it’s difficult for me to do the problems, but my group mates helped me to figure it out. Here, dikw stands for data, information, knowledge, and wisdom. Reflection on Unit 2

In Unit 2, I studied the BRIS, where I explored the wide range of topics that helped me to understand how mathematics and computers are connected. At the beginning, it seemed technical, but gradually they became meaningful as I practiced on them. I have learned about the number systems, that is, binary, decimal, and hexadecimal conversions. Initially, it was difficult and confusing, but with practice, it was easy. I got to know how computers actually perform calculations at a fundamental level. Another topic that grabbed my attention was fun with images. I know that what an image is, but not in detail, not more. learning that images are made up of pixels, each pixel holds a value, was fascinating. It changed my mind about how I looked at digital images. I learned how image quality depends on image resolution. It was challenging for me to understand how flipping or reisizing happens based on logical operations. Moving ahead, I knew that optimization and formal analysis helped me think logically, instead of solving problems directly. These topics I found difficult and confusing. The Bridges of Konigsberg problem was good but not simple. It showed how real-world puzzles led to developement of the graph theory. Finally, learning about the abstraction and modeling taught me how to simplify complex problems. Overall, I say that this kind of learning improved my problem-solving skills at some point.
